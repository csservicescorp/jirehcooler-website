South Florida heat doesn't leave much room for a slow AC repair. When your system stops cooling, blows warm air, or starts making noises it shouldn't, our technicians diagnose the actual problem, not just the symptom, and give you a written estimate before any work begins.

- Refrigerant leaks and low-charge systems
- Failed capacitors, contactors, and motors
- Compressor and condenser issues
- Frozen evaporator coils
- Electrical and control-board faults
- Airflow and ductwork restrictions

## Signs Your AC May Need Repair

- The system is running but not actually cooling the space
- Warm air is coming from the vents
- Short cycling — the unit turns on and off more frequently than usual
- Unusual noises, such as grinding, squealing, or banging
- Weak or uneven airflow from room to room
- Higher-than-normal humidity indoors
- A sudden increase in your energy bill with no other explanation
- Ice forming on the indoor coil or refrigerant lines

If you notice any of these, it's worth having a technician look at the system before a small issue turns into a bigger repair.

## What to Expect During Your Service Visit

A technician inspects the system to find the actual cause of the problem, not just the symptom you're noticing. Once we know what's wrong, you get a written estimate for the repair before any work begins. If parts are needed, we'll explain what they are and why. ## Repair or Replace?

Not every problem calls for a new system, and not every repair is worth making on an aging unit. When we diagnose your AC, we'll give you an honest read on where your system stands, including factors like its age, repair history, and efficiency, so you can decide with real information instead of guessing. If a repair is the right call, we'll make it. If a replacement makes more sense long-term, we'll walk you through what that looks like, including [financing options](/financing/) for qualified customers.
